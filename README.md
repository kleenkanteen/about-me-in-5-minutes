Being curious: Just naturally who I am I guess. In 4th grade I took apart a dollar store dancing flower that had a small solar panel on it. I took apart the whole thing and the small electic motor that made the flower move and connected the wire to the battery to it and saw how a electric motor worked. But when I hooked it up, it spinned for just one 1 rotation and then stayed in place. Readjusted, same results. I left it and didn't think about it again. Years later, in grade 11 physics, we were taught how a electric motor works. I remember it I still didn't understand why when I played with one years ago, it only spun once and not continuously. I went home that day and on youtube watched a couple videos explaining how a eletric works, and it still didn't make sense why it kept on spinning. Finally I found a video of someone actually explained it properly and it made perfect sense. It was a subtle but important detail. You can ask me this and even now will be able to answer: What allows a electric motoro to spin continuosly instead of just a partial rotation? Passing electricity through the inner coil creates a magnetic field around it, and there's a also a magnetic field made by the outer magnets in the motor. The magnetic fields are in conflict with each other so this causes a spinning force. So the inner coil spins until it reaches the balanced state of the magnetic fields of the inner coil and outer magnets being aligned. But once it reaches this state, why doesn't it stay there like how I observed long ago? Why does it go beyond and spin further than 1 rotation, and forever? I don't think most of my classmates would be able to understand, especially from the class lesson taught to us. I wanted to know and found out myself.

I'm detailed so I'll disect an argument and belief and question someone about it. I don't believe things blindly, I'll ask for sources. I only use reddit, no instagram at all, since reddit is a text heavy platform while instagram doens't even let you make text only posts. I try to be logically rigorous and provide sources for my claims. I don't want to say I'm a skilled debater because that just sounds like bragging. But I'm passionate about finding the truth and having the most evidence based beliefs, and I've deveploed my debate and questioning skills to help me in that pursuit.
