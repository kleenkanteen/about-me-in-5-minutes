Words that describe me, some might sound like bragging but I don't mean to:
- Curious - I ask a lot of questions according to others. I also ask google a lot of questions and have gotten pretty good at it by using the search queries. Some things I've realized about questions: 
  - The world is like a dark cave and questions is the illuminating flashlight. We are all born knowing nothing. We learn by asking.
  - A good question is like a well directed arrow, it gets to the flesh of the matter. Good questions get you the right information you want or need from someone. Wrong question, wrong answer.
- Active - In class I ask questions and on the online class discussion boards I ask and answer questions. This isn't just in programming classes but also in others like religion and geographic info systems. When a class is 100 people then it's not realistic for everyone to ask. But in the 20 person classes taught by 3rd/4th year teaching assistants, that's a good time to ask questions. Consistently I've seen very few few students be active and ask questions in the 20 person classes. I like asking questions about something I don't understand or want to understand further.
- Bold - Natural tendency to speak my mind if I disagree with something. Also helps with asking questions because I believe many are socially nervous to ask questions like raising their hand and speaking in a class of 100 people, but I don't feel that because I want to learn and that's all that matters to me.
- Balance - I'm not the best at it but the older I'm getting, the more I'm realizing balance is important. Don't talk too much or too less. Sleep too much or too less. Think too much or too less. Find the golden zone.
- Humor - There's been a lot of situations where I cracked a joke that made in sense in the moment and others laughed. Whenver I'm in a group project I say a joke now and then when the moment is ripe. I like the art of humor, lightens the mood and helps you bond with with group members.
- Programming - What I like about programming is the practical impact. I can make a simple but useful website, and millions of others can use and benefit it instantly. If you design a car, it's expensive to recreate it every time. Instant replication with software is amazing. The internet lets you scale very easily. What I like about the craft itself is what Linus Torvalds, the creator of Linux, said: it's a blend of art and engineering. There are elegant solutions and ugly solutions.
- Social - In the second half of 3rd year, an idea came to my head. This was when classes were mostly online, so when people asked questions or said things in the zoom chat, I could see their name. There were students in class who are actively engaging, which I like seeing, but I never talked to them before. And there were others I worked with in group projects but never had a 1 on 1 chat. So I emailed them, their uni emails were based off their name which I could see, and asked if we could talk about school, programming, and internships, great common topics to talk about. I did this with 15 students, in Dec 2021 (winter break) and July-Aug 2022, when exams were done and most of them and me were a month or 2 into our 12-16 month internship after 3rd year. They were all 3rd year in least one of my classes. Most of them were people I never talked to before and we were able to have interesting conversations 1-2 hour long conversations about school, programming, internships, and even other topics like how to learn, a side project they did, what they are learning on the side. I feel like most students don't do what I did, reaching out to fellow classmates they never met before just for a casual chat, which is easy too since you can know their name from the zoom classes and shoot them an email to schedule a virtual coffee chat and guarantee their eyeballs will read it. It's a great way to socialize and have interesting convos.
- Never Bored - I cannot remember the last time I got bored. There is always an article I have on to-read list or a course/thing to learn on my to-learn list. The amount of things I want to do and read outstrip the available time.
- Questioning - I don't believe things blindly, I will ask how you why something is true
- Debate - If I disagree I lay out my points and reasoning
- Logically rigorous - I think over arguments and make sure they make logical sense, so convincing me is not super easy
- Honest - Very important when debating, a dishonest debater will never find the truth. Now I can't say that I'm honest for sure but I try to engage and understand other's perspectives.
- Fan of practical philosophy and practical pschology, a good website about these is effectiviology.com
- Being honest - Related to the above point, I'll be honest in another way, about myself. I don't want to say all good things about me in this post so I'll say a flaw: 
- Before univesity and even during university, I didn't develop strong discipline. For all of high school and most of university, till the end of 3rd year, my sleep schedule was bad. I would not get enough sleep some nights then oversleep other nights to compensate.  In first year I didn't enjoy calculus, I found it boring and mechanical, so I didn't consistently do the calculus homework and did badly on the first midterm. I thought my calculus knowledge from grade 12 would be enough but university calculs was on a different level. Just attending lectures wasn't enough as the prof's lectures weren't good, he went too fast and I couldn't understand even though I learnt the stuff in grade 12. I had to fix my act and start doing the homework consisently and read the textbook to teach me, which it did very well since it's been reviewed so many times, and my grade therefore rebounded. Discipline is not something I've established before university, or even during university, too hectic and I get burnt out. Finished my 3rd year in Apr 2022 and am on a internship from then for a year, it's much more relaxed with fixed working hours unlike school so I am able to make a lot more progress with my discipline. I am trying to improve and made a to do list and a daily schedule to solidify it. I have a backlog of things to learn, many programming courses/mini courses and ideas I want to bring to life, and I understand how important discipline and being consisent is to acheive them. I am trying to match my huge learning ambitions with my work ethic.
